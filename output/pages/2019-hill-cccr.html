Hill 2019, “Theories for Past and Future Monsoon Rainfall Changes”

The paper focuses on the three dominant theoretical paradigms for understanding monsoon precipitation and its changes: convective quasi-equilibrium (CQE), the moist static energy (MSE) budget, and monsoons as shifts in the Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ). [This alphabet soup of semi-rhyming acronyms led to an original cheeky title of “CQE, MSE, ITCZ, or all three?” I thank the reviewers (Mike Byrne and one anonymous) for convincing me that was a bit /too/ cheeky.]

The paper also discusses challenges the community must surmount in inferring monsoon rainfall behavior in Earth’s past via paleoclimate proxy records. It concludes on a positive note, emphasizing the ability of theory to directly inform model development efforts and promoting recent advances in how theory and modeling results can be most effectively communicated to societal stakeholders.
