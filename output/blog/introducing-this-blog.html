Intro

In this inaugural post, I’ll introduce myself, explain my motivations for creating this blog, and list some topics of interest that I intend to post on in the future. My goal is to do roughly one post per month.

To borrow and amend a phrase from /This American Life/, this blog acknowledges the existence of human-induced climate change. This will largely be taken as given; there are many other places online to find flame-wars on the topic. I have enabled a comment system, which I will moderate as necessary to keep discussion on topic and civil.

Who I am

I am a postdoctoral scientist at the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory of Columbia University. I did my Ph.D. work in the Program in AOS at Princeton University, where my adviser was Dr. Yi Ming.

Why this blog

There are several existing blogs on weather/climate that I recommend, all of which by scientists with much more expertise than me.

Nevertheless, as evidenced by the list of topics below, I will span a broader range of topics than any of the existing blogs do. I hope that this will create a rich space, but given the hyper-specific combination of interests, there is a danger in this becoming a purely self indulgent exercise.

Topics

  1. Constraining past and future hydrological changes
  2. The climate science software toolchain
  3. Open science, reproducible research, etc.

  4. Climate and weather in music and other media

    I am slightly obsessed with music.

  5. Science outreach and K-12 education

    I am currently working with Jon Aurnou and other collaborators at UCLA on a build-it-yourself rotating table platform for conducting demonstrations of rotating fluid dynamics, which are central to the dynamics of Earth’s atmosphere, ocean, and interior as well as the atmospheres of other planets.
